Human Development

More Nations Improve, but Many People Still Struggle

Increasing Nations

From 1980 to 2013, more nations saw their Human Development Index rise, reflecting improvements in health, education, and their standard of living. In 1980, Niger ranked the lowest of nations with an HDI of 0.191 and Australia ranked at the top with 0.841. By 2013, Niger pulled itself up to 0.337, mimicking the overall world trend as the world's HDI average moved up to an HDI of 0.70. More and more nations rose in human development.

Counting People, Not Nations

While the number of nations have risen in the HDI measurement, the improvement masks the fact that the world's population is unevenly distributed. China and India, which has more than 2.6 billion people, make up about 37% of the world's population. The HDI scores for China and India are improving, but India in particular, with a 2013 HDI of .586 has many people who still need to see progress.
